Timelines are based upon Anno Mundi (the beginning of the world). Thousand year markers are placed to show where a thousand year falls.

The world history shown here reflects the Dead Sea Scroll calendar dates. The DSS calendar documentation says that there are 7,000 year of world history. Each 1,000 year mark represents one day of creation, thus 7,000 year history. At the end of the 7,000 year, the world will be destroyed and a new earth will be created.

Each year is represented by a pixel on the screen in the X-axis.

The goal of the timeline is to be able to physically view the events in history as they occurred.

A marker for the current year is programmatically generated so we can always see the current year on the timeline.

Goals

To be able to filter people shown on the History graph by hiding them. As the first people from Adam on are hidden, the goal is to adjust the timeline to begin with the first shown person.

Add more historical people to provide a relationship to the historical person and the timeline of the Bible.
