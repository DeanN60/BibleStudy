Dr. Thomas Ice explains the difference between Literal vs. Allegorical Interpretation in this paper.

I believe that Scripture interprets Scripture meaning that I hold to a literal interpretation of Scripture. Yes, there are allegories in scripture, but it is clear that the text is an allegory.

For example in , Daniel is using allegory to describe a ram and a goat. Daniel is explaining a dream and what the objects in the dream mean. Because we know this is a dream we know that this is not literal.

In we see a literal creation of the earth. This passage is describing God's spirit hovering over the surface of the waters. We know that this is literal because this is not a dream and is a physical description of the first day of creation.

We get into problems when people try to interpret the Bible in their own way. When our life experiences are used to interpret the Bible we get into problems. Someone who lived around the time of Christ would not understand a written description of an aircraft flying overhead. If the description said that the aircraft had 100 people onboard, they would probably think that the aircraft had eaten the people. They are not aware of any mechanical man made craft which is able to fly. They should interpret it as literal even thought they do not understand how it could be.

We get into the same situation when we read and we try to interpret the four living creatures.

  • Has anyone seen a creature with flashes of light like lightning around them?
  • Has anyone seen a creature with four faces?
  • Has anyone seen a creature with four wings?
  • Has anyone seen a creature with feet which look like a calves hoof?

Yet we know this is a literal description because of the detail given. We have never seen a creature like these, just like someone in the time of Christ has never seen a aircraft flying like we are used to seeing every day.

Problem with only Allegorical Interpretation

The problem with allegorical interpretation is each person will interpret the same passage differently. In the past, some have used this to control their flock. They say that only trained people can understand the Scriptures.

God gave the Scriptures so we all could understand it. The New Testament was written in "Common Greek" so the common person would be able to read it.

The book of Revelation is often considered allegorical in nature because of the imagery used. Yes, there are allegorical passages in Revelation, but we are told in the first chapter that we will be blessed if we read it and keep the things which are written in it. How can we do this if it is allegorical and hard to understand? The early church understood Revelation clearly because they knew the Old Testament.
