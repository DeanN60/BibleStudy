Calvinism came out of the reformation. It was founded by John Calvin. John Calvin was born in 1509 and died in 1564.

John Calvin followed the teaching of St. Augustine of Hippo. St Augustine of Hippo was born November 13, 354 AD, in Thagaste. St Augustine died August 28, 430 AD in Hippo Regius.

It is important to note that the early church knew nothing of Calvinism and therefore never taught it.

Single vs Double Predestination

Single predestination holds to God choosing only who is saved. It makes no mention of predestining anyone to hell.

Double predestination holds to God choosing who is saved and who is destined to go to hell.

When you look at single vs double predestination they are exactly the same. The only difference is that the single predestination view hides the fact that those who are not saved go to hell.

TULIP

It was after John Calvin's death that the acronym TULIP was developed and used to help remember what the 5 points of Calvinism are.

  • Total Depravity
  • Unconditional Election
  • Limited Atonement
  • Irresistible Grace
  • Perseverance of the Saints
