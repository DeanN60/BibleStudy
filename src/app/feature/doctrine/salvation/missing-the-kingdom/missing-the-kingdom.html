I Never Knew You

In it talks about people who have done good work in the name of Jesus. But Jesus tells them that He never knew them.

To know someone means that they have a deep intimate knowledge of someone not just that they know of them.

The key in this passage is that the people were depending upon their works as proof that they were saved. It clearly says that they should have obeyed God. This means that God's plan of salvation comes only through His Son Jesus Christ.

The Parable of the 10 Virgins

speaks of 10 virgins with lamps. These lamps were more like torches than a small lamp. The reason it mentions that the foolish virgins did not take any oil with them is that a jar of oil was needed to keep the torch lit and it was an extra container that they had to carry. The torch had rags on the end which were soaked in the oil. When the torch went out, they had to soak the rags on the torch in the oil to replenish the oil that was burned off. Then they had to light it from one of the other's torches. The wise virgins did not have enough to share because the amound of oil that they brought was enough for one night only and would not last if they had given some to the foolish virgins.

It is important to note that the virgins did not know when the bride groom would come. There was no set time of his arrival. They had to be ready at all times.

The Parable of the 10 Talents

In it talks about talents being distributed to servants. We assume that these servants are saved, but are they? To the one servant was given 5 talents, to another 2 talents and to the last servant 1 talent.

We see that the first two servants doubled what they had. The last servant went and hid his talent in the ground. He went about his own business and gave no thought to the master's business.
