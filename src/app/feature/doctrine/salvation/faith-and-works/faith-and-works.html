An old story explains this the best way possible. A tight rope walker was walking on a tight rope stretched over Niagara Falls. He had a wheel borrow and asked the public if they believed he could push the wheel borrow across the falls on the rope. They all cheered and said "YES". Then he asked for a volunteer to get into the wheel borrow. The crowd went silent. You see they really didn't believe that he could push the wheel borrow across the falls on the rope. After a moment of defining silence a person stepped forward and climbed into the wheel borrow.

The crowd said that they believed he could push the wheel borrow across the falls on the rope. But when it came down to actually doing it, only one person really believed.

Do you believe that Jesus paid the debt of your sin? Many claim to believe, but few actually put their trust in Him. They show their trust by how they live. This is what James was talking about when he said that faith without works is dead.
